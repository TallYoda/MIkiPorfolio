Biography

Mikiyas Sintayehu was born in 1998 in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. He earned his bachelor degree in 2023 in Interdisciplinary Visual Arts from the Addis Ababa University Alle School of Fine Arts and Design. He also holds a diploma in Fine Arts from Entoto Polytechnic College, Addis Ababa (2018).

In 2022 Mikiyas had his first solo show entitled Reflections at Atmosphere Gallery, Addis Ababa, After that he had a couple more solo Exhibitions in Addis Ababa, Escape at Social Addis, 2023, and the last and recent Odyssey at Artawi Gallery, 2024. He has also participated in group exhibitions held at different venues in Addis Ababa, including at the Brazil in Addis Cultural Space (Cube, 2023);Alliance Éthio-Française Gallery (Impression of Time, 2023); Hyatt Regency Hotel (Faces, 2021); AAU, Alle School of Fine Arts and Design Gallery (The Tales of Rodari, 2020); Entoto Polytechnic College (Graduation Exhibition, 2018).

Mikiyas Sintayehu is a studio artist based in Addis Ababa. Through his experimental paintings, Mikiyas continues to explore the effects of technology on the social life and personal space of people mostly of his generation.

Artist Statement

My inspiration often begins with seemingly ordinary moments—friends gathered in my studio, engaged in conversation, or an acquaintance scrolling away on their phone. These quiet, everyday scenes, for reasons I’m still discovering, spark a deep need to capture them. I take a photograph to preserve the moment, and from there, the process of transferring it onto canvas begins. It’s in this act of painting that a larger narrative or meaning starts to reveal itself, emerging from every detail of the scene. Emotions, stories, and interpretations I hadn’t considered at the outset begin to unfold, surprising me as much as they might surprise the viewer.

My work reflects the spirit of being a young creative today, capturing the rhythm of life as I experience it. Using an Impressionist approach, I focus on light, atmosphere, and perspective to evoke the emotional depth of my subjects, with human figures at the heart of my compositions. I aim to share these personal yet universal moments, inviting the audience to glimpse into my world—the joy, challenges, and connections that shape my life and those around me.

In sharing my experiences with authenticity, I recognize that my art is inherently political. By portraying my desires, needs, and the world as I see it, I engage with the broader societal context. My art becomes a reflection of the collective struggles and aspirations of those around me. It’s through capturing these fragments of an infinitely complex and beautiful world that I use my artistic language to share something meaningful with anyone willing to look closely.
